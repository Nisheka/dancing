Sabaragamu dances (Sabaragamuwa Natum)- Sri Lankan Classical Dance

History

Sabaragamuwa Dance has its roots in the Ratnapura District and various areas of the Kegalle District. The dance method is practiced across scattered regions, with Klonne Koralaya, Dekoralaya, and Kukulu Koralaya being prominent areas associated with Sabaragamuwa Dance.

Musical Instrument:

The primary musical instrument used in Sabaragamuwa Dance is the Dawula. This instrument holds a unique significance as it is one of the few instruments that still employs dual action, involving both hands and Kadippu (attached sticks).

Dressing Style:

The characteristic attire worn during Sabaragamuwa Dance is known as "Saman Devi Andum Kattalaya" or the cold Sabaragamuwa Dressing. This attire contributes to the unique visual appeal of the dance and reflects the cultural identity of the region.
