Kandyan Dance - Sri Lankan Classical Dance

Kandyan dance (Sinhala: උඩරට නැටුම්) encompasses various dance forms popular and native to the area called Kandy of the Central Hills region known as Udarata in Sri Lanka, which have today spread to other parts of the country. It is an example of Sinhalese culture in Sri Lanka.

Costume in Kandyan Dance:

Dancers don elaborate costumes, with a prominent feature being the Ves costume. The attire includes a decorative beaded net on the chest and a headdress that adds height. The headdress has a metallic front, and anklets worn produce a distinct metallic rattle.

Music Accompaniment:

Kandyan Dance is traditionally accompanied by percussion instruments. The Geta Beraya, a unique drum, takes the lead. To maintain rhythm, dancers use small cymbals known as Thalampota.
