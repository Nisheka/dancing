Folk dance- Sri Lankan Classical Dance

History

Folk dances in Sri Lanka hold cultural significance and are closely linked to traditional activities and celebrations. They offer a glimpse into the local way of life and heritage.

Leekeli (Stick Dance):

The Leekeli, or stick dance, is a captivating folk dance that involves rhythmic movements with sticks. It likely originated from rural communities and is often performed during festive occasions to entertain and engage the audience.

Kalagedi (Pot Dance):

The Kalagedi, or pot dance, is a vibrant folk performance where dancers skillfully balance pots on their heads while dancing. This dance is not only an artistic display but also a reflection of the dexterity of local craftsmen.

Raban (Hand Drum) Dance:

The Raban dance involves skilled drummers who play the raban, a hand drum, while dancing. This rhythmic performance is closely tied to traditional celebrations and showcases the rhythm and musicality inherent in Sri Lankan culture.
