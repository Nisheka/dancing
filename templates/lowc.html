Low Country dances (Pahatharata Natum)- Sri Lankan Classical Dance

Kolam

Kolam, a unique performing art in Sri Lanka, traces its roots to various groups and families across the country. Key areas include Ambalangoda, Bentara, and Matara. The dance flourished from 1800 to 1950, serving both entertainment and spiritual purposes. However, modernization and lack of sponsorship led to its decline.

Kolam Dance and Mask:

The sole Conservation Center for Traditional Kolam Dance and Mask in Sri Lanka, sponsored by the government, resides in the ancestral home of the Tukka Wadu Generation in Maha-Ambalangoda.

Components of Kolam Dance:

Kolam Dance encompasses various elements, including: General Characters of Monarchy: Depicting individuals from historical monarchies. Imaginary Characters: Showcasing unique and distinct dancing styles. Popular Legends and Folktales: Incorporating well-known stories from local traditions. Religious Stories: Linking narratives to Buddha's previous lives and spiritual teachings.
